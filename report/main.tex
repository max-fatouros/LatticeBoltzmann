\subsection{Lattice Boltzmann}
The phase-space density function is defined as $f(\mathbf x, \mathbf p, t)$.
The probability of finding a particle with position $\mathbf x_{0}$, momentum $\mathbf p_{0}$, at time $t_{0}$, such that
\[
  (\mathbf x, \mathbf p, t) < (\mathbf x_{0}, \mathbf p_{0}, t_{0}) < (\mathbf x + \delta \mathbf x, \mathbf p + \delta \mathbf p, t + \delta t)
\]

is given by $f_{i}(\mathbf x,\mathbf p,t)\delta \mathbf x \delta \mathbf p \delta t$.

The LBM method discretizes the system to get $f(\mathbf x, \mathbf p_{i}, t)$, where $\mathbf x$ and $t$ are now defined on a lattice, and $\mathbf p_{i}$ is a set of $M$ discrete momenta that the particles can have.


The unit vectors in the direction of $\mathbf p_{i}$ are defined as $\mathbf e_{i}$.

To go from individual discrete quantities, to continuous macroscopic observables, we take the moments of the phase-space distribution
\[
  \rho(\mathbf x, t) = \sum_{i} f(\mathbf x, \mathbf p_{i}, t)
\]

\[
  \rho(\mathbf x, t)  \mathbf u(\mathbf x, t) = \sum_{i} \mathbf e_{i} f(\mathbf x, \mathbf p_{i}, t)
\]

Where $\rho$ is the mass density and $\mathbf u$ is the velocity of the ensemble of particles at a lattice point.








The number of spatial dimensions $N$ and the number of discrete momenta $M$ are often characterized by a naming scheme as DNQM. Therefore, a system in 2 dimensions with 9 discrete momenta is denoted as D2Q9.

\subsubsection{D2Q9 Model}
The D2Q9 models defines the directions $\mathbf e_{i}$ as shown in~\Cref{fig:direction-d2q9}.
\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.5\textwidth]{directions_d2q9}
    \caption{The directions of the unit vectors in the D2Q9 model.}
    \label{fig:direction-d2q9}

The weights associated with these directions are~\cite{bottcher_computational_2021}
\[
  w_{i} =
    \begin{cases}
      4/9 & i = 1 \\
      1/9 & i \in 2,\ldots,5 \\
      1/36 & i \in 6,\ldots,9 \\
   \end{cases}
\]
\subsubsection{D3Q15 Model}
The D3Q15 model defines the directions $\mathbf e_{i}$ as shown in~\Cref{fig:direction-d3q15}.

The weights associated with these directions are~\cite{arumuga_perumal_review_2015}

\[
  w_{i} =
    \begin{cases}
      2/9 & i = 1 \\
      1/9 & i \in 2,\ldots,7 \\
      1/72 & i \in 8,\ldots,15 \\
   \end{cases}
\]
