\subsection{Lattice Boltzmann}
The phase-space density\footnotemark{} function for our system of particles is defined as $f(\mathbf x, \mathbf v, t)$.
\footnotetext{
  I am refraining from calling $f$ a probability density function, and $F$ a probability mass function, since they can only be normalized properly at fixed slices through time.
}
The number\footnotemark{} of particles with position $\mathbf x_{0}$, velocity $\mathbf v_{0}$, at time $t_{0}$, such that
\begin{equation}
  (\mathbf x, \mathbf v) < (\mathbf x_{0}, \mathbf v_{0}) < (\mathbf x + \delta \mathbf x, \mathbf v + \delta \mathbf v)
\end{equation}
is given by $f(\mathbf x,\mathbf v, t_{0})\delta \mathbf x \delta \mathbf v$.
\footnotetext{
  For any fixed value $t=t_{0}$, $f(\mathbf x, \mathbf v, t_{0})$ can be thought of as either a probability density function, or a particle-number density function.
  The two interpretations can be converted between one another by multiplying $f$ by a constant normalization factor over all parameters.
  This factor would have no effect on the calculations to follow.
}

The LBM discretizes the system to get the discrete density function
\begin{equation}
F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t) = \int_{\mathbf x - \frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathbf x}^{\mathbf x + \frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathbf x}\int_{\mathbf v - \frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathbf v}^{\mathbf v + \frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathbf v}\int_{\mathbf x - \frac{1}{2}\Delta t}^{\mathbf x + \frac{1}{2}\Delta t} f(\mathbf x', \mathbf v', t') \delta \mathbf x' \delta \mathbf v' \delta t'
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf x$ and $t$ are now defined on a lattice, and $\mathbf v_{i}$ is a set of $M$ discrete velocities that the particles are constrained to have.

$\mathbf x$, $\mathbf v_{i}$ and $t$ are now to be thought of as defining the centre of a bin of particles with bin-volume $\Delta \mathbf x \Delta \mathbf v_{i}\Delta t$.
The number of particles in the bin is then given directly by $F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)$.

The unit vectors in the direction of $\mathbf v_{i}$ are defined as $\mathbf e_{i}$.

To calculate the macroscopic observables of interest, we take the moments of the phase-space density
\begin{equation}
  \rho(\mathbf x, t) = \sum_{i} F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
  \rho(\mathbf x, t)  \mathbf u(\mathbf x, t) = \sum_{i} \mathbf v_{i} F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
\end{equation}

Where $\rho$ is the number of particles at lattice point $(\mathbf x, t)$ the and $\mathbf u$ is the average velocity of the ensemble of particles at lattice point $(\mathbf x, t)$.


We'll adopt the notation $F_{i} = F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)$. At every given point on our lattice, we can compute the equilibrium density function as~\cite{bottcher_computational_2021}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:equilibrium}
  F^{\textnormal{eq}}_{i}
  = w_{i}\rho
  \left[1
    + \frac{3}{c_{s}^{2}}\mathbf u \cdot \mathbf v_{i}
    + \frac{9}{2c_{s}^{4}}(\mathbf u \cdot \mathbf v_{i})^{2}
    + \frac{3}{2c_{s}^{2}}\mathbf u^{2}
  \right]
\end{equation}
The weight term $w_{i}$, and the rest of~\Cref{eq:equilibrium} can be found by an expansion of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution in Hermite polynomials~\cite{bottcher_computational_2021}.

We can then \enquote{collide} the particles, to move them one step closer to their equilibrium distribution
\begin{equation}
  F^{*}(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
  = F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
  - \frac{\Delta t}{\tau}
  \left[
    F^{\textnormal{eq}}(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
    - F(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
  \right]
\end{equation}

after that, we \enquote{stream} the particles, moving each density bin $F_{i}(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)$ in the direction of its velocity via
\begin{equation}
  F_{i}(\mathbf x + \mathbf v_{i}, \mathbf v_{i}, t + \Delta t)
  = F^{*}(\mathbf x, \mathbf v_{i}, t)
\end{equation}

\[
  w_{i} =
    \begin{cases}
      4/9 & i = 1 \\
      1/9 & i \in 2,\ldots,5 \\
      1/36 & i \in 6,\ldots,9 \\
   \end{cases}
\]
